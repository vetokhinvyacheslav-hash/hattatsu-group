# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Manufacturing and industrial enterprise leaders in Russia and CIS: production directors, operations managers, HR directors, plant managers. They are time-constrained, skeptical of consultants, and evaluate credibility before anything else. They read case studies, look for named clients, and care about measurable results. They arrive via referral or specific search, not brand campaigns.

## Product Purpose

Generate qualified B2B leads for Hattatsu Group's consulting, training, gamification, and HR services. The site must establish expert authority fast and route visitors to one of two conversion paths: the 5-minute production maturity diagnostic, or a direct consultation request. Secondary goal: showcase the team's depth (8 practitioners with real enterprise histories) and the client roster (AGC, Barilla, PelikanGlass).

## Brand Personality

Expert, practical, reliable. No hype, no fluff. The voice is a senior practitioner explaining to a peer — direct, evidence-backed, slightly understated. The brand earns trust through specifics (numbers, named clients, named methodologies) rather than adjectives.

## References

McKinsey.com and Korn Ferry — restrained, authoritative, white-dominant, strong typography hierarchy, data-forward. The reference is the approach, not the aesthetic; Hattatsu is warmer and more direct than McKinsey.

## Anti-references

- SaaS and startup landing pages (bright orange CTAs, gradient heroes, "transform your workflow" copy)
- Russian corporate sites from the 2010s (cramped, dark navy, dated grid)
- Generic consulting template sites (stock photo heroes of handshakes, vague mission statements)
- Edgy/maximalist brand aesthetics — this audience reads theatrics as a red flag

## Design Principles

1. **Specificity over claims**: every value proposition backed by a number, a client name, or a methodology. Never just "we're the best."
2. **Expert restraint**: authority comes from not overselling. Calm, controlled visual weight. Whitespace is credibility.
3. **Evidence-first hierarchy**: case studies, stats, and team bios are first-class content, not buried at the bottom.
4. **Clear conversion paths**: every page should make the diagnostic or consultation CTA feel like the obvious next step, not an interrupt.
5. **Practice what you preach**: a consulting company's site must itself demonstrate operational discipline — fast, clean, precise, no waste.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 Level AA. Russian primary language. Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
